Value-at-Risk (VaR) como medida de riesgo

\[ Pr(Loss > VaR) = p \]

\[ \begin{aligned} Pr( -R_{PF} > VaR) &= p \quad \Leftrightarrow \\ Pr( -R_{PF} > VaR) &= p \end{aligned} \]

\[ \begin{aligned} Pr(R_{PF,t+1} < -VaR^{p}_{t+1}) &= p \quad \Leftrightarrow \\ Pr(R_{PF,t+1}/\sigma_{PF,t+1} < -VaR^{p}_{t+1}/\sigma_{PF,t+1}) &= p \quad \Leftrightarrow \\ Pr(z_{t+1} < VaR^{p}_{t+1}/\sigma_{PF,t+1}) &= p \quad \Leftrightarrow \\ Pr(R_{PF,t+1} < -VaR^{p}_{t+1}) &= p \quad \Leftrightarrow \\ \Phi(-VaR^{p}_{t+1}/\sigma_{PF,t+1}) &= p \end{aligned} \]
